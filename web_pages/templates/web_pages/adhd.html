AD/HD Support for adults

In his book, (italic)"Scattered Minds", Dr Gabor Mate, who himself has a diagnosis of AD/HD, uses a metaphor to describe the differences in the way neurodiverse people experience how their brains function, compared to neurotypicals. Imagine all the streams of thoughts in your brain as lanes in a busy traffic intersection. For neurotypicals, there is a vigilant traffic cop, directing and ordering the traffic to avoid chaos and conjestion. In the case of the those with AD/HD, that traffic cop is fast asleep. This is a frenetic and exhausting image of the common lived experience of those with AD/HD, and why getting the right support and coping strategies is imperative for well-being.

Some of the common areas of concern for adults with ADHD include:

  • Feelings of overwhelm, irritability, anxiety, and depression
  • Sense of failure and low self-esteem
  • Difficulties with motivation
  • Continuously starting new tasks before finishing old ones
  • Procrastination
  • Inability to focus or prioritise
  • Rejection sensitivity
  • Time-blindness
  • Forgetfulness
  • Experiencing emotions intensely
  • Difficulty regulating emotions
  • Relationship / Social anxieties and challenges
  • Impulsivity
  • Finding planning and organizing stressful and overwhelming
  • Often feeling misunderstood and unsupoorted
